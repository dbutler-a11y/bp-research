The Problem

Blood Pressure Is a Symptom, Not the Disease

Your body isn't broken. It's responding to fixable conditions.

High blood pressure means your blood vessels are stiff or inflamed, your nervous system is stuck in fight-or-flight, your blood chemistry is imbalanced, or your kidneys aren't regulating fluid properly. Medications manage the number on the cuff. This protocol fixes what's producing it.

The Framework

Four Pillars of Blood Pressure Reversal

Each pillar targets a distinct root cause. Together, they restore the body's own regulation.

Pillar 1

Fix the Pipes — Vascular Endothelial Repair

Restore nitric oxide production, reverse arterial calcification, reduce arterial stiffness.

  • L-Citrulline — Nitric oxide precursor. Arteries relax and dilate. (-4 to -8 mmHg)
  • Raw Garlic / Aged Garlic Extract — Allicin boosts NO, thins blood. (-8.3 mmHg in clinical trials)
  • Vitamin K2 (MK-7) — Pulls calcium OUT of arteries into bones. Reverses stiffness.
  • Magnesium Glycinate — Natural calcium channel blocker. Relaxes artery walls. (-3 to -8 mmHg)
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) — Reduces vascular inflammation, improves elasticity.
  • Beetroot / Beet juice — Highest dietary nitrate source. Alternative NO pathway.
  • Sunlight — UV releases stored nitric oxide from skin. 20-30 min daily.
Pillar 2

Calm the System — Autonomic Nervous System Reset

Reduce sympathetic overactivation, increase vagal tone, lower cortisol.

  • 4-7-8 Breathing — Inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s. Activates vagus nerve. (-7 mmHg SBP)
  • Cold Exposure — 30-60s cold water. Triggers mammalian dive reflex.
  • Humming / Chanting / Prayer — Vibrations stimulate vagus nerve through larynx.
  • Ashwagandha (KSM-66) — Reduces cortisol by ~30%. Adaptogen.
  • Sleep 7-8 hours — Kidneys reset sodium during deep sleep. Non-negotiable.
Pillar 3

Balance the Chemistry — Blood Chemistry Optimization

Correct electrolyte ratios, resolve insulin resistance, optimize hydration.

  • Potassium: 3,500-4,700 mg/day — From avocado, coconut water, spinach, sweet potato. Neutralizes sodium sensitivity.
  • Eliminate refined sugar & carbs — Reduces insulin. Can drop BP 10-20 mmHg alone.
  • Intermittent Fasting (16:8) — Restores insulin sensitivity. Breaks sodium retention loop.
  • Hydration — Half body weight (lbs) in ounces of water + electrolytes daily.
Pillar 4

Support the Filters — Renal & Hepatic Function

Optimize kidney sodium handling and liver hormone metabolism.

  • Hibiscus Tea (3 cups/day) — Clinically proven: -7.2 mmHg SBP at 6 weeks. Up to -13.2 in severe cases.
  • Dandelion Root Tea — Natural diuretic. Doesn't deplete potassium like pharma diuretics.
  • Vitamin D3 (5,000-10,000 IU) — Regulates RAAS. Always pair with K2.
  • Fermented Foods — Gut bacteria produce SCFAs that regulate blood pressure.

Important: Medication Interaction Warning

Garlic, magnesium, and hibiscus tea have pharmacological-level blood pressure effects and may potentiate existing antihypertensive medications, potentially causing blood pressure to drop too low. Share this protocol with your doctor before starting. Monitor BP closely. Never stop or reduce prescribed medication without your doctor's approval.

The 90-Day Plan

Phased Implementation Timeline

Don't do everything at once. Build progressively.

1

Foundation

Days 1 – 30
  • Eliminate refined sugar, processed food, and excess refined carbs
  • Start magnesium, K2, D3, and garlic
  • Hibiscus tea 3x daily
  • 4-7-8 breathing twice daily
  • Hydration protocol with electrolytes
  • Increase potassium-rich foods at every meal
  • Daily BP log (morning + evening)
2

Amplification

Days 31 – 60
  • Add L-Citrulline, Omega-3, Ashwagandha
  • Daily beetroot or beet juice
  • Cold exposure (30-60s cold shower)
  • 20-30 min daily sunlight
  • Begin intermittent fasting (16:8)
  • 30-min daily walk
  • Add dandelion root tea
3

Optimization & Medical Review

Days 61 – 90
  • Full protocol running — all pillars active
  • Add fermented foods (kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir)
  • Grounding: 20 min barefoot on grass/earth daily
  • Schedule physician review with 60-day BP log
  • Request bloodwork: fasting insulin, hsCRP, Mg RBC, Vitamin D
  • Discuss medication tapering if BP consistently <130/80
